5 Ways to Stand Out as a Freelance Writer in 2025

Real stories. Real transformation. Discover the strategies that actually work.

Let's Get Real for a Minute...

You know that moment when you're staring at your screen, waiting for an email reply from a client who ghosted you? Or worse, refreshing job boards, watching dozens of proposals with zero replies?

Yeah… I've been there too.

Back in 2023, I had only one consistent writing client—paying me peanuts. Every week, I sent out pitch after pitch, customizing each one. But nothing. I even questioned if I was cut out for this whole freelance thing. I'd scroll LinkedIn and see people bragging about "hitting $5k months," and I'd feel like an imposter.

If you've ever felt invisible in the freelancing world...

This post is for you. I'm not here to throw vague advice. I'll walk you through 5 things that actually helped me stand out—and keep clients coming back for more.

Let's talk like freelancers at a coffee shop, yeah?

Strategy #1

Tell Your Story — Because That's Your Brand

Here's the deal: people remember stories, not titles. You're not just a "freelance writer." You're someone who left a draining 9-5 to pursue passion. Or someone who learned copywriting during power outages with a borrowed laptop. That's powerful.

"Clients don't just want a service—they want a human."

When I started sharing bits of my personal journey on my portfolio site and LinkedIn, the responses shifted. One client even said, "I hired you because I felt like you got it—you've been where our audience is."

Action Tip:

Dedicate a section on your website that tells your journey. Not a corporate bio. Be real. Vulnerability builds trust faster than a polished resume ever will.

Strategy #2

Stop Competing. Start Positioning.

Here's an ugly truth:

Thousands of writers offer "SEO blog posts" or "copywriting services." If that's all you're saying, you're blending in, not standing out.

So how do you position?

Think about the exact transformation you offer. For example:

"I help B2B SaaS brands turn technical jargon into stories that convert."

"I write content that ranks and reads like your favorite Netflix show."

The moment I niched down to "tech ghostwriting for founders," my inbox became less about "how much?" and more like "can we book a call?"

Action Tip:

Update your LinkedIn headline, Upwork title, or personal website with a positioning statement that screams, "This is what I do differently."

Strategy #3

Get Found Where Clients Are Searching

In 2025, it's no longer enough to just "be available." You've got to show up where decision-makers hang out.

My LinkedIn Transformation

One of the biggest shifts for me came when I stopped hiding. I started posting weekly on LinkedIn—even if I had no likes. I shared writing tips, behind-the-scenes of my process, and lessons from tough client experiences. Slowly, I became visible. People started tagging me. Referrals happened.

I know it's scary. But visibility isn't vanity. It's strategy.

Action Tip:

Pick one platform (LinkedIn is gold for writers) and commit to showing up once or twice a week. Don't overthink it. Just share what you're learning.

Strategy #4

Show Proof. Not Just Promises.

Clients are done reading, "I'll deliver high-quality articles." Everyone says that.

What they want to see is proof.

SEO Results

A screenshot of an article ranking on Google.

Testimonials

A testimonial from a happy client.

Case Studies

How your copy helped increase conversions.

"When I started using social proof—even just a kind DM or email reply—it made a difference. One founder told me, 'I could tell people trusted you, and that's why I did too.'"

— Personal Experience

Action Tip:

Create a simple "Proof" section on your portfolio. Add screenshots, data, testimonials—anything that shows you don't just write, you deliver.

Strategy #5

Make Clients Feel Something

This one might be the most important.

Great writers don't just inform—they make people feel. If you can make your clients feel heard, respected, and excited to work with you, you're not just a writer—you're unforgettable.

The Thank-You That Changed Everything

I remember once, after a particularly chaotic project, I sent the client a handwritten thank-you email with a Loom video explaining how I approached their brand tone. That tiny gesture? Got me 3 referrals.

In a world of AI tools and automation, being human is your biggest edge.

Action Tip: Be the freelancer who...

Responds warmly
Delivers early
Adds thoughtful touches
Asks good questions

It's never "just business." Relationships are the business.

Final Thoughts: You're Closer Than You Think

Listen, I know how easy it is to feel like just another name in a sea of writers. But here's the truth that took me two years to believe:

There's space for you. But you have to show up as YOU.

Not the perfect version. Not the "hustle 24/7" version. Just you—with your story, your quirks, your values, and your care for the craft.

Freelancing isn't a lottery. It's a path. One where you build brick by brick—client by client.

So if you're reading this and you've been doubting whether you'll ever stand out...

I want you to bookmark this moment. Because this right here might just be the start of your best chapter yet.

Go get 'em. 🚀
